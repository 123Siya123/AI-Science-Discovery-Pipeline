### 2. Copper's Antimicrobial Surface Enhancement via Thermal Cycling
**Domain:** Materials Science / Public Health
**Problem:**
Copper surfaces kill bacteria within hours (the "oligodynamic effect"), but the
mechanism slows as copper oxide forms. Hospitals can't afford to constantly polish
copper surfaces. Could a simple thermal cycling process (heating and cooling
copper) create a specific surface micro-texture or oxide pattern that ACCELERATES
antimicrobial action instead of hindering it?
**Challenge:**
Find the optimal heating temperature (oven: 150-250Â°C) and cooling rate (air vs.
water quench) for copper sheets that maximizes antimicrobial surface area and
maintains a self-renewing oxide layer. The result would be a free, DIY protocol
for making "super-antimicrobial" copper door handles, surfaces, and water vessels
for communities without access to healthcare.
**Home Test:** Heat copper sheets/pipes in oven at different temps, cool differently.
Test antimicrobial performance by placing bread/fruit on treated vs untreated copper.

### 3. Thermoelectric Power from Waste Heat (Heater/Stove Gradient)
**Domain:** Energy Physics / Thermodynamics
**Problem:**
Enormous amounts of heat are wasted (heaters, stoves, car engines, industrial
exhausts). Thermoelectric generators (TEGs) convert temperature DIFFERENCES into
electricity (Seebeck effect), but commercial TEGs are expensive. Can a DIY

**Challenge:**
Determine the optimal junction geometry, number of series-connected thermocouples,
and temperature differential to generate enough power from a heater or stove to
charge a phone (5V, 500mA = 2.5W). Focus on maximizing the Seebeck coefficient
through wire gauge, junction treatment (acid etching? Oxidation?), and thermal
isolation between hot and cold sides.
**Home Test:** Build a copper-iron thermocouple array. Place hot side on heater/
stove, cold side in air/water. Measure voltage with multimeter. Iterate on
geometry to maximize power output.

### 6. Electromagnetic Seed Treatment for Enhanced Germination
**Domain:** Agricultural Biophysics
**Problem:**
Millions of small farmers struggle with poor germination rates. Research shows
that exposing seeds to specific electromagnetic fields BEFORE planting can
increase germination rate by 10-40% and accelerate early growth. The mechanism
(ion channel activation? Aquaporin stimulation?) is not fully understood.
**Challenge:**
Determine the OPTIMAL electromagnetic exposure for common crop seeds (beans,
corn, tomatoes). Variables: frequency (static magnet vs. alternating field from

**Home Test:** Wind copper wire around a cardboard tube to make a coil. Connect
to battery/power supply. Place seeds inside for various durations. Plant treated
vs. untreated seeds and track germination rate and growth over 2-3 weeks.

### 7. Solar Water Desalination Efficiency Optimization
**Domain:** Thermodynamics / Environmental Engineering
**Problem:**
Solar stills (evaporate saltwater, condense freshwater) are ancient but inefficient
(~30-40% solar efficiency). Modern research shows that dark, porous materials
floating on the water surface can achieve 80%+ efficiency by localizing heat at
the evaporation interface instead of heating the bulk water.
**Challenge:**
Design an optimized solar still using ONLY household materials that maximizes
distillation rate. What is the best dark absorber (burnt cloth? Charcoal? Black
sponge)? What wick structure draws water to the hot surface fastest? How should
the condensation surface be angled and cooled? Target: produce drinkable water
from seawater/contaminated water at maximum rate per unit area.
**Home Test:** Build test stills using plastic containers, black fabric, plastic
wrap. Measure water output per hour. Compare bulk-heating vs. surface-heating
designs. Test with salt water â€” measure salt content of output.

### 12. Acoustic Levitation & Manipulation with DIY Ultrasonic Arrays
**Domain:** Acoustics / Physics
**Problem:**
Ultrasonic transducers (from cheap distance sensors ~$1 each) can create standing
waves that levitate small objects (foam beads, water drops). This is used in
contactless manufacturing and drug delivery research. The physics of optimal
array geometry for HOME-BUILT levitators isn't well explored.
**Challenge:**
Determine the minimum number of ultrasonic transducers and optimal arrangement
to reliably levitate and MOVE objects along a controlled path. Can a DIY array
made from cheap HC-SR04 sensor transducers, driven by an Arduino, manipulate
droplets of liquid for contactless mixing (poor-person's lab-on-chip)?
**Home Test:** Extract transducers from HC-SR04 sensors (~$1). Build a phased
array driven by Arduino. Map the acoustic field with a microphone. Attempt
levitation of styrofoam beads and water drops. Test object manipulation by
phase-shifting the drive signals.
